## Glossary of terms:

- **NFT:** Non-Fungible Token, a unique and indivisible digital asset that is stored on a blockchain, typically used for things like digital art or collectibles.
- **Metadata:** Additional information associated with an NFT, such as its name, description, or image.
- **Publisher:** The entity responsible for creating and managing metadata for NFTs.
- **Soulbound:** A relationship between two NFTs that ensures they are always owned together and can only be transferred as a pair.
- **Meta NFT:** A special type of NFT that serves as a container for metadata associated with another NFT.
- **Fragment:** A type of NFT that encapsulates discrete pieces of metadata and can be transferred separately from its parent Meta NFT.
- **Fragment Manager:** A smart contract that manages the creation, ownership, and transfer of Fragments.
- **Standard:** A specification or set of guidelines that defines how something should be implemented or used.
- **Protocol:** A set of rules or procedures that govern how different entities interact with each other.
- **Smart contract:** A self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement between buyer and seller being directly written into lines of code.
- **Ethereum:** A decentralized, open-source blockchain platform that supports smart contracts and the development of decentralized applications (dApps).
